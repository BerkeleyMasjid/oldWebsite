The Berkeley Masjid Foundation

The Berkeley Masjid Foundation (BMF) is established as a not-for-profit religious organization that is incorporated in the State of California.  Located in Berkeley, CA, BMF strives to increase Islamic awareness while providing a communal focal point for Muslims and those interested in Islamic ideology. 

BMF seeks to establish permanence so as to support a growing population of Muslims in Berkeley, CA and surrounding cities in the San Francisco Bay Area. This permanence includes a masjid within the City of Berkeley that not only features Islamic education, scholarship, and cultural outreach, but also provides space that embodies the Islamic ideals of peace, tolerance and enlightenment. 

BMF serves as a milieu for the young.  A significant amount of BMF’s energies and resources are used to support youth and young adult activities.  These youth and young adults include university / international students and scholars who have permanent ties to locations outside of the San Francisco Bay Area.

Brief Chronology of BMF

1990 - Formally organized.

1992 - Incorporated as a non-profit organization.

1990-1993: Established leasehold at 228 Fulton Street, Berkeley, CA

1993-1995: Established leasehold at 2510 Channing Way, Berkeley, CA

1995-1999: Established leasehold  at 2434 Dwight Way, Berkeley, CA

1999-2004: Established leasehold at 2366 San Pablo Ave, Berkeley, CA

From 1990 onward, operational fees and leasehold obligations, were covered by donations largely gathered during Friday prayer services.  Between 1999 and 2005 the BMF also arranged for Friday prayer services at the Young Women’s Christian Association (YWCA) on Bancroft Avenue, across from the UC Berkeley campus.   


Present: Permanent ownership interest at 2718 Derby Street, Berkeley, CA
