Berkeley Masjid Loan Repayment Obligation

Berkeley Masjid prioritizes servicing its loan obligation of approximately $600,000.  This obligation is an aggregation of interest-free loans that private citizens generously offered to meet building construction and related costs.  BMF services these loans via the donations of its generous supporters, special donation requests during Jummah congregational prayers, and regular fundraising activities.

The mosque exists because of your support.  Your support may come in different ways.  The first is through a tax-deductible financial pledge to BMF.  Your pledge may be made check, Paypal, SquareSpace, or a cash contributions.  You may offer a single, monthly, or annual pledge. You may also help sponsor a fundraising event or help with on-going fundraising efforts.

Zakat

Zakat


Offering charity and support is one of the five (5) pillars of Islam, and an obligation on all able Muslims.  Charitable offerings range from food donations to the poor, to support for and maintenance of mosque, to help for those who actively struggle to preserve the religion. 

The consensus for offering charity is that one should give 2.5% of his or her resources as acquired over the preceding twelve lunar months.  These resources typically include income.


While not regarded as a Zakat offering, volunteer service may constitute a sacred form of giving.


During the month of Ramadan, Muslims also make contributions to persons who are poor, as well as to entities that serve the needs of the poor, elderly, sickly, and orphaned.
